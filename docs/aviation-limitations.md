# Aviation limitations

Visual observability depends on camera angle, calibration, resolution, lighting, rain, snow, glare, occlusion, markings, stand geometry, aircraft type, equipment appearance, uniforms, and model coverage.

Chocks, beacon state, towbar state, fueling, bridge connection, door state, passenger activity, service completion, and pushback readiness may be unavailable or ambiguous. A generic truck cannot establish fueling or catering. A person detector cannot establish employment, authorization, intent, or unsafe conduct.

The application distinguishes direct observation, model prediction, rule inference, manual correction, and unavailable data. Near-miss output is a monocular candidate. Image-space movement is not real-world speed. No output should accuse a person, authorize movement, replace a marshaller or ramp controller, or override airport procedures.

> AeroRamp Vision is an operational decision-support and analytics platform. Production deployment requires airport-specific validation, camera calibration, safety assessment, cybersecurity review, privacy review, legal review, and approval from relevant airport and aviation authorities.
